Training rhythm Legs, arms, control, cardio, recovery. No max lifts, no loaded twisting, no pushing through back pain.

Back-care rule: this plan should be cleared by Lyric's pediatrician, orthopedist, or physical therapist. Stop the session for sharp pain, nerve symptoms, dizziness, or back pain above 2 out of 10. If she has brace instructions, those instructions win.

Rules

  • Keep ribs stacked over hips and move slowly enough to own each rep.
  • Use light dumbbells or bands. Add load only when form and pain stay calm.
  • Skip max lifts, heavy barbell squats, heavy deadlifts, sit-ups, loaded twisting, loaded jumps, box jumps, and depth jumps.

Weekly Schedule

  • Best rhythm: Monday, Wednesday, Friday.
  • Second option: Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday.
  • Keep one rest day between strength sessions.

Progression

  • Weeks 1-2: learn form, easy resistance.
  • Weeks 3-4: add one rep per leg and arm set or slightly stronger band.
  • Weeks 5-6: add one set to one leg/arm pair or add 2 cardio intervals.

Equipment

  • Mini-band or long resistance band.
  • Light dumbbells for curls, rows, presses, carries, and leg work.
  • Stable chair, low step, basketball, and water.
